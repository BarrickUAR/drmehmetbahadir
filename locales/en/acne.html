Acne

Diseases / Acne
Acne Problem

The earliest sign is the increased sebaceous secretion rate, which causes the skin to appear oily (seborrhea). It is often accompanied by comedones or blackheads, typically appearing on the sides of the nose and forehead. Comedones are follicular plugs, formed from hardened sebum and follicular waste material. In severe cases, nodules turn into fluid-filled cysts. These are pseudocysts due to the absence of epithelial layers. This is considered severe acne. 01

The most affected areas are the face (cheeks, chin, nose, forehead), neck (back side), chest, and shoulders. 02

The natural tendency of acne is to heal spontaneously, but the exact timing of healing is uncertain. Acne usually disappears by age 25, but it may last longer in some individuals. Acne improves in the summer due to sun exposure and salty water. However, heat does not contribute to healing. In fact, hot and humid climates can worsen acne, turning it into an inflammatory and exudative condition. 03

Scar tissue refers to the scars formed on the skin after injury or skin diseases. During the healing process, it is a result of functional skin tissue being replaced by connective tissue. 04

Acne is seen in approximately 70% of the population during adolescence, but only 10% require medical assistance. For these patients, seeking medical help is crucial to prevent scarring, especially on the face. It may affect their psychological and social life. Medical treatment and long-term support are required for these individuals. The main histological feature is severe inflammatory folliculitis. Early stages show follicular plugs. Later, inflammatory cell accumulation, histiocytes, and multinucleated granulomatous reactions occur. In long-term lesions, fibrous tissue accumulations trigger scar tissue formation. It is believed that important inflammatory lesions in acne form due to follicle rupture. 05

In recent years, thanks to the Ministry of Health's support for phytotherapy in Turkey, acne can be treated with side-effect-free phytotherapeutic applications. These treatments are easy to apply at home, providing significant improvement in about 6 weeks without any side effects. 06
