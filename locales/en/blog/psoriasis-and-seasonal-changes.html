Psoriasis and Seasonal Changes: Protecting Your Skin Health in Winter and Summer

Psoriasis is a chronic skin disease that causes flaking, redness, and itching on the skin. However, one of the factors that affect the progression of the disease is seasonal changes. In winter, skin dryness, cold weather, and low humidity can worsen psoriasis symptoms, while in summer, sunlight, warm weather, and sweating may have different effects.

As seasons change, people with psoriasis may need to review their skincare and lifestyle habits. In this article, we will discuss how psoriasis is affected by seasonal changes and what steps should be taken to maintain skin health in different seasons.

Psoriasis in Winter Winter is generally a more challenging season for people with psoriasis. Cold weather, dry environments, and low humidity can cause skin to dry out and become irritated. This can make psoriasis symptoms more pronounced. The main reasons for psoriasis worsening in winter are as follows:

Cold Weather and Skin Dryness: Cold weather causes skin to lose moisture. Especially heated rooms and air conditioning reduce the moisture in the air, making the skin even drier and more sensitive. Dry skin can cause psoriasis to become itchier, more flaky, and red spots to appear.

Insufficient Sunlight: UV light is often beneficial in the treatment of psoriasis. However, in winter, there is less sunlight, so patients may not receive the full benefits. Lack of sunlight can lead to an increase in psoriasis symptoms.

Cold Weather and Cracks: Cold weather can cause the outer layer of the skin to crack. These cracks can trigger psoriasis and make the healing process more difficult.

Tips for Managing Psoriasis in Winter Use of Moisturizer: It is extremely important to moisturize your skin in winter. You can choose heavy, oily moisturizers to protect your skin from the dry air. Natural moisturizers like aloe vera and shea butter can also be helpful.

Moderate Water Temperature: Taking a warm bath can prevent your skin from drying out. Otherwise, hot water can dry out the skin even more and worsen psoriasis.

Benefit from Sunlight: It may be difficult to get sunlight in winter, but spending time outside on sunny days can reduce inflammation on the skin. Of course, be sure not to overdo it.

Balancing Temperature and Humidity: Home ventilation systems can dry out the skin. Using humidifiers can help maintain the skin's moisture balance.

Skincare: During the winter months when your skin is more sensitive, avoid soaps containing harsh chemicals. Choose soft, fragrance-free soaps and skin-friendly cleansers.

Psoriasis in Summer Summer may be a more comfortable season for some people with psoriasis, as sunlight can help improve the condition. However, hot weather, sweating, sunburn, and other challenges of summer can also affect psoriasis. Common problems faced by people with psoriasis in the summer include:

Exposure to the Sun: Sunlight can help psoriasis improve. UV rays suppress excessive cell growth and reduce inflammation. However, excessive sun exposure can lead to sunburn and worsen psoriasis.

Hot Weather and Sweating: Hot weather causes the body to sweat. Excessive sweating can increase skin irritation and trigger psoriasis.

Sunburn: Psoriasis-prone skin is more sensitive to the sun. Burns can cause the disease to become more severe.

Sea and Chlorine-Free Water: Chlorine in pools can have a drying effect on the skin and worsen psoriasis symptoms. However, seawater may be healing for some psoriasis patients, as salty water can reduce inflammation on the skin.

Tips for Managing Psoriasis in Summer Sun Protection: Always use sunscreen with a high SPF (30 or higher) before going out in the sun. Avoid prolonged direct exposure to the sun.

Control Sweating: To prevent excessive sweating, prefer cool environments and take frequent showers to cleanse sweat. Also, make sure your skin is able to breathe.

Dress Lightly: In hot weather, choose cotton and comfortable clothing. Synthetic fabrics can trap sweat, which may worsen psoriasis.

Swimming and Water Activities: If you enjoy swimming in the sea, seawater can help with psoriasis. However, remember that chlorine in pools can dry out your skin.

Skin Cleansing and Moisturizing: During the summer, with increased sweating, pay more attention to skin cleansing. Use light moisturizers to maintain your skin's moisture balance.

Conclusion: Coexisting with Psoriasis Through Seasonal Changes Psoriasis can be affected by seasonal changes, but with proper skincare and lifestyle habits, it is possible to adapt to these changes. In winter, keeping your skin moisturized, and in summer, protecting it from the sun, are important steps for managing your condition. Remember that everyone’s skin is different, so adapting your seasonal skincare to your personal needs will yield the best results.

If your psoriasis worsens due to seasonal changes, it would be beneficial to consult with a dermatologist and review your treatment process. With the right care and attention, your skin can be protected in every season.
