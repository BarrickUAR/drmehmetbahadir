Psychological stress and distress can trigger psoriasis or increase existing rashes in predisposed individuals. Therefore, psychological help for highly stressed individuals can reduce the severity of the disease. Additionally, microbial infections such as tooth decay, tonsillitis, urinary tract infections, trauma like friction, impact, scratching, excessive alcohol consumption, certain metabolic diseases (hypocalcemia), and medications can also act as triggers. 01

Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR states that psoriasis flares up in children with streptococcal throat infections. Therefore, patients with recurrent tonsillitis should receive complete treatment. Dermatologists often request a throat culture and antibiotic sensitivity test even if the patient has no complaints. Additionally, treating chronic infections like tooth decay is essential. 02

Psoriasis decreases in sunny climates and increases in winter. It is also less common in low-altitude regions. In dry climates, patients experience more itching due to low humidity. Therefore, patients may feel more comfortable in humid climates. In winter, heating systems dry the air, increasing itching. Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR recommends using moisturizers frequently in winter to prevent flare-ups. 03

Injury to non-psoriatic skin can lead to new psoriatic lesions in that area, known as the Koebner phenomenon. Sunburn and cuts can cause this. While sunlight is beneficial for psoriasis, excessive sun exposure should be avoided. Psoriasis patients should avoid permanent tattoos as they can trigger lesions. Scratching can also lead to new lesions, so treatment is essential. 04

Studies show that psoriasis changes during significant life events. Recent research indicates that stress increases certain substances in the blood that trigger the immune system. Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR emphasizes that psoriasis is more common in women during adolescence, pregnancy, and postpartum periods due to hormonal changes and increased stress. 05

Psoriasis can occur at any age in men but is not as specific as in women. Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR notes that men often develop psoriasis during periods of personal stress, such as financial difficulties or family losses. University exams and military service are also common triggers. 06

Cupping, a treatment method used by the Prophet Muhammad (PBUH), has been recognized by the Ministry of Health in integrative medicine. However, there is no scientific evidence that cupping helps with psoriasis. Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR advises against cupping, as it can worsen psoriasis lesions. 07

Rheumatism, once considered a disease of the elderly, now affects younger individuals and even children. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial. Common symptoms include joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and limited mobility. Patients should seek proper treatment and maintain morale. 08

Psoriasis commonly affects the arms, legs, scalp, and elbows but rarely the face. Dr. Mehmet İlteber BAHADIR explains that facial psoriasis can result from improper skincare products, tattoos, or piercings. Proper skincare and avoiding harsh chemicals are essential. 09

Ankylosing spondylitis patients should maintain proper posture while working at a computer. Using ergonomic chairs, adjusting screen height, and taking regular breaks can help reduce pain and stiffness. Stretching exercises during breaks are also beneficial. 10

Eczema is a chronic skin condition characterized by itchy, red, and inflamed lesions. Nutrition plays a significant role in managing eczema. Anti-inflammatory foods like salmon, avocado, and olive oil can help reduce symptoms. Probiotic-rich foods like yogurt and kefir support gut health and may alleviate eczema. 11

Rheumatism, once considered a disease of the elderly, now affects younger individuals and even children. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial. Common symptoms include joint pain, swelling, stiffness, and limited mobility. Patients should seek proper treatment and maintain morale. 12

Psoriasis patients should wear tightly woven, cotton clothing that retains moisture. Avoid thick, woolen sweaters that dry out the skin. Layering with body-hugging garments can help maintain skin moisture and prevent flare-ups. 13

Rheumatic diseases cause pain, swelling, and limited mobility in joints, muscles, and bones. Diagnosis involves physical examination, imaging, and laboratory tests. Early diagnosis and treatment can prevent further damage. 14

Inflammatory rheumatism (Rheumatoid Arthritis) not only damages joints but can also lead to vascular complications like arteriosclerosis if untreated. Patients often experience fatigue, pain, and depression. Early treatment is essential to improve quality of life. 15
