Skin Diseases

Psoriasis

Psoriasis is a disease that causes redness and rashes on the skin. It affects around 1 million people in Turkey. Despite its appearance, it is not contagious. People with psoriasis are often excluded from social life due to the physical symptoms.

Psoriasis can be classified into different types based on its characteristics. The most common type is the plaque type, which causes redness on the knees and elbows. Other types include inverse, erythrodermic, and guttate psoriasis, which are classified based on the severity and location of the symptoms.

Eczema

Eczema is a type of skin disease that has no serious health risks. It is quite common, affecting 1 in 12 people.

Eczema causes itching, but it is generally not too troublesome. Its only effect is that it can cause cosmetic concerns if it appears on certain parts of the body.

Lichen Planus

Lichen Planus is an inflammatory skin and mucosal disease with an unknown cause, but it is strongly immunopathogenic. It typically appears as a purplish-colored, flat, itchy ring-shaped lesion, often with a white lacy surface. It can appear in several spots or in large numbers.

In 30% of cases, there is a white mesh pattern on the mucous membranes in the mouth, and lesions may also appear in the genital area.

It may cause hair loss on the scalp in some cases. Treatment typically involves topical corticosteroids to relieve itching, although the disease may resolve on its own.

Ichthyosis

Ichthyosis is a skin disorder characterized by the abnormal accumulation of keratin, resulting in scaly patches. These scales are most noticeable on the body’s extremities, such as the elbows and knees, but may also affect the arms, legs, and hands.

The condition can be congenital or acquired, and its treatment generally involves moisturizing agents and sometimes keratolytic agents. Ichthyosis can be caused by a variety of underlying conditions, including metabolic disorders and certain genetic factors.

Ichthyosis vulgaris, commonly known as "fish scale disease," is the most well-known type. The scales form due to the accumulation of dead skin cells that cannot be shed properly.
