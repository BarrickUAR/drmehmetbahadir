Acne

Diseases / Acne
Acne Problem

The earliest sign is an increase in sebaceous secretion speed, which makes the skin appear oily (seborrhea). Typically, comedones or blackheads appear with the oily appearance, mostly around the sides of the nose and forehead. Comedones are follicular plugs composed of hardened sebum and follicular waste material. In severe cases, nodules turn into cysts with fluctuation. These lesions are actually pseudocysts because they lack epithelial layers. This is considered severe acne. 01

The most affected areas are the face, cheeks, chin, nose, forehead, neck, back, chest, and shoulders. 02

The natural course of the disease is to heal on its own. However, it is unpredictable when acne will heal. Acne typically disappears by the age of 25. However, in some patients, it may persist much longer. Acne tends to improve in the summer, with sunlight and saltwater helping many patients. However, warmth does not improve it, and truly hot and humid climates may cause acne to become inflamed and exudative. 03

Scar tissue refers to the scars that form on the skin after an injury or a skin disease. During healing, functional skin tissue is replaced by connective tissue, which is identified as a sign. 04

Acne is seen in about 70% of the population during adolescence. However, only 10% of these people need medical assistance. It is crucial for these patients to seek medical help. Otherwise, the person's face can become covered with scar tissue. This can affect the person's psychological and social life. Medical treatment and long-term support therapy are necessary for these patients. Histologically, the main feature is an excessively inflammatory folliculitis. In the early stage, follicular plugs can be seen. Later, there is an accumulation of inflammatory cells, histiocytes, and multinucleated giant granulomatous reactions. Long-lasting lesions have fibrous tissue deposits that initiate scar formation. It is thought that significant inflammatory lesions in acne result from follicular rupture. 05

Thanks to the importance given by the Ministry of Health to phytotherapy in recent years, acne can now be treated with completely safe and effective phytotherapeutic applications with no side effects. These applications can easily be performed at home by individuals. After an average of 6 weeks of application, individuals can easily get rid of all acne lesions. A life without acne is possible without using any medication with side effects. 06
